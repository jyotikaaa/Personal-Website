Can't just code - a story
Can't just code

This is Joy. Joy works 9 to 5 in a company where people barely interact with each other (except for when they got errors in their code, ofcourse). She gets herself some coffee during breaks and sips it alone on her desk, cursing her mundane job and wishing that she should have followed her dream career instead, architecture.

At last, she decides to try and switch companies a couple of times and if this feeling of being doomed and being unable to love her job persists, she'll quit programing and try something else out.

So now she's back to square one, spewing her resumes and CVs in companies that have better work policies (or so she heard). Finally, she lands a job in this new company which her friend had recommended once. "I know you and I've worked in that company before. You'll love it there, trust me", was what he had said. So putting her faith in him, she gave it a shot.

Well, at this point, she doesn't know how everything in her life is about to change.

This company, is nothing like she expected it to be. Nothing here is about just getting things done. Rather than opting for traditional ways of doing stuff, they call in for meetings for everyone to brainstorm together and come up with different yet top-notch ideas. There's always someone guiding the rest of the team towards making better decisions by sharing their experiences of failure and success. Funny stories of how things went wrong in the past and how they got their way around it.

Here's her favourite part, they have got a centralized music system! They believe music greatly affects the quality of the atmosphere in the room and the work being done as well. Someone's feeling tired? They got Bon Jovi's 'It's my life' blasting to refuel them. Don't feel like working today? They'll get Rihanna & Drake to convince you to work.

There's a perennial flow of ideas and information and exceptional vibes across the office everyday at work. It's exciting as it brings the best out of her. It's not just working your ass off anymore. It's about being a part of a community that believes in growing together.

And suddenly while working today, when the room was quite except for 'Some Nights' by Fun playing in the background, she experienced this epiphany that now, she loves her job as a software developer. And life feels right and much more better as she gets back to work.

If you have read this far, greetings to you too! I'm Jyotika (aka Joy). So now you know what I mean when I say I can't just code. Like Selena said it,
